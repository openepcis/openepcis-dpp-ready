GS1 JSON-LD → EN 18223 Digital Product Passport

What this shows: how a product passport written as good GS1 Web Vocabulary JSON-LD, addressed by a GS1 Digital Link, becomes a CEN/CENELEC EN 18223 Digital Product Passport.

What to do: pick a real example on the left (one per EU/US delegated act: battery, electronics, textile, deforestation, packaging, construction, detergent, food), or paste your own, then press Derive. EN 18223 defines two plain-JSON serializations of the passport; use the View dropdown on the right to switch between them:

  • EN 18223 expanded (Annex A): every data point is a DataElement with a dictionaryReference into the ref.openepcis.io dictionary and a valueDataType from the ontology. granularity comes from the Digital Link Application Identifiers (01 → model, 01+10 → batch, 01+21 → item), and contentSpecificationIds from the dictionaries the payload uses.
  • EN 18223 compressed: the same passport as plain key-value JSON (clauses 5.2.6 to 5.2.9).

Both outputs are ordinary JSON. EN 18223 happens to call them expanded and compressed, which is a separate thing from JSON-LD expansion: the input on the left is the JSON-LD, the output is plain EN 18223 JSON. Every example and context is bundled, so it all runs in your browser with no network.
